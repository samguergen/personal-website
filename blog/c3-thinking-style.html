Thinking Style

After taking the Gregorc Thinking test, which we were advised to take at the beginning of our Dev Bootcamp experience, I found out that I was an Abstract Sequential Thinker. This means that I love the world of theory and abstract thoughts, unsurprising since I was a Philosophy major.

This thinking style eventually revealed itself after analyzing my pattern of thinking through the HTML/CSS and Ruby exercises that I completed so far. I noticed, from my notes, wireframes and pseudocodes, that before even writing down my code, I will tend to focus on the 'bigger' picture first: What am I trying to achieve? What will be the general logic and control flow of my method?

These are questions I think about before even thinking about the difference situations, or types of inputs and outputs that my method will receive. So far, this type of thinking style proved to be rather helpful and quite compatible with the "programmer" way of thinking, but there is always room for improvement. For example, the input and output of your method is something to constantly keep in mind, not only after thinking through the logic.
