Thinking Style

After taking the Gregorc Thinking test, which we were advised to take at the beginning of our Dev Bootcamp experience, I found out that I was an Abstract Sequential Thinker. This means that I love the world of theory and abstract thoughts, unsurprising since I was a Philosophy major.

This thinking style eventually revealed itself after I took some time to analyze the pattern of thinking that I used in my HTML/CSS and Ruby exercises completed so far. From my notes, wireframes and pseudocodes, I noticed that before writing down my code, I will first focus on the 'bigger' picture: what am I trying to achieve? What will be the general logic and control flow of my method?

These are questions I think about before looking at the different real-world examples that my code will be used for, or different types of parameters that it will receive. So far, this thinking style proved to be rather helpful and quite compatible with the "programmer" way of thinking, but there is always room for improvement. For example, the parameters, or inputs and outputs of my methods is something to constantly keep in mind, not only after thinking through the logic.

-Sam
