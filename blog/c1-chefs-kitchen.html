12/22/2014

Dev Bootcamp is an intensive learning experience, where we will be spending a lot of hours coding and learning new concepts. However, it won't just be about sitting in a class and listening to teachers- pairing and working together will be just as important. Pairing is not just a Dev Bootcamp thing- it's an essential part of learning to be a programmer- as the nature of software engineering requires you to be strongly collaborative.

After watching the Fireside Chat, I was positively surprised by DBC's focus on personal growth - and not just about learning the curriculum as a 'purchased package'. DBC wants us to notice how this program will make us change as people, and how this change will affect our personal life and professional career in the long-run.

Let's face it. We are all in it together, investing all of our time, effort and money in this intensive program, and we expect it to pay off. Sooner or later, we will begin to struggle, to cram up a lot of information at once, and at times we may feel insecure about our ability to make it through. But the anxiety will also be beneficial, because it will teach us about our own limits, about maximizing our learning, and how to take out the best of this experience. DBC wants us to notice every step of that change.

DBC winks that a big part of the experience is also about learning more on our own and practicing, practicing and practicing. Let the games begin- time to "bit" the bullet :)

-Sam
